"I'm doing pretty good as far as geniuses go."

A leap into electronic maximalism. Teaming with Takashi Murakami for the iconic cover, Kanye launched Dropout Bear into stadium-sized soundscapes. The album outsold 50 Cent's Curtis in a historic showdown, moving 957,000 copies in week one.

"My mother was my everything."

Born from the devastating loss of his mother Donda and a broken engagement, Kanye abandoned rapping entirely. Armed only with Auto-Tune and an 808 drum machine, he crafted a frozen landscape of grief. Drake, Kid Cudi, and Travis Scott all trace their DNA back to this record.

"No more cap."

Named after his late mother. Three stadium listening events became cultural spectacles — Kanye living inside Mercedes-Benz Stadium, the full-body Balenciaga mask becoming his new identity. The album art: a pure black square. The mask never came off.

"True strength requires no announcement."
